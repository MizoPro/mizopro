Snow's Cholera Data

Description

During the 1854 cholera epidemic in London, John Snow collected data on the location (street addresses) of persons who died of cholera, as well as the location of water pumps in the city. Snow believed (correctly) that cholera was transmitted through contaminated water. Snow's map of London, showing the locations of deaths and water pumps is given below:

The table below gives the coordinates of 578 person who died of cholera. The data are a digitized version of Snow's map, with coordinate relative to a set of axes through the center of the map, produced by the National Center for Geographic Information and Analysis. The units are meters. The data have been modified by rounding to the nearest 0.1 meter.
